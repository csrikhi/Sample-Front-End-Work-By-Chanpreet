Reasons For Chronic diseases

Causes of Chronic Disease?

Chronic diseases are caused due to the environmental (manmade) and genetic factors. We can't do anything about risk factors like age, family history, and ethnicity. However, fortunately, we can take care of some risk factors like diet, physical activity, stress, alcohol and smoking.

Unhealthy Diet

Unhealthy diets, especially those which have a high content in fats, free sugars and salt is one among some of the leading causes of chronic diseases like cardiovascular diseases (CVD), type 2 diabetes and certain cancers. 2.7 million Deaths are attributable to diets low in fruits and vegetables.

→Worldwide, low intake of fruits and vegetables is estimated to cause
→Migration is generally associated with higher caloric intake and higher intake of refined and processed grains.
→The typical Asian Indian diet is high in carbohydrates (70–80% of total daily caloric intake), and low in protein (9–10% of total daily caloric      intake).
→Various researches have shown that Asian Indians diet is mainly deficient in Vitamin D, Vitamin B6, Vitamin B12 and Pantothenic acid.

Low physical activity levels

1.9 million Deaths are attributable to physical inactivity Worldwide. At least 60% of the global population does not achieve the minimum recommendation of 30 minutes of moderate intensity physical activity daily.

→In a study on physical activity majority of Asian Indian adults were inactive during their leisure time
→Asian Indians have been shown to be less physically active when compared with other ethnic groups.

Smoking

→Smoking increases blood pressure, decreases HDL (good) cholesterol, decreases exercise tolerance and increases the tendency for blood to     clot.
→Smoking also increases the risk of recurrent coronary heart disease after bypass surgery.
→Cigarette smoking combined with a family history of heart disease also seems to greatly increase the risk.

High Stress Levels

→Stress is known to contribute to heart problems and increase risk of death due to high blood pressure and less resistance to disease     including heart disease.
→Increased stress levels can lead to depression
→Depression is also a risk factor for heart disease, and has been associated with diabetes and metabolic syndrome.
→Asian Indians are already at high risk of diabetes. Excess stress increases the release of the stress hormone cortisol, which can further increase blood sugar and accelerate progression to diabetes. Effects of Stress on your Body

High on Alcohol

→According to the American Heart Association drinking too much alcohol can raise the levels of some fats in your blood. It can also lead to high blood pressure, heart disease and an increased calorie intake, which can lead to other complications. Want to get your facts right about alcohol effect on Brain?
