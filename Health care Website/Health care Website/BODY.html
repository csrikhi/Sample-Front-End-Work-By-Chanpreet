BODY

Weight of Asians Indians may not appear to be as overweight as other ethnic groups. But, may have inherited a gene that makes their bodies store more fat around their stomach, a factor referred to as central obesity. This type of body fat is linked to a higher rate of diabetes and heart attacks. The typical body shape of Asian Indian men who is at a high risk for these conditions involves a large stomach with relatively slender arms and legs.

Ideal weight

Waist to Hip ratio


Studies have shown that measurements that involve abdominal fat are more accurate than BMI in assessing heart attack risk in Asian Indians. One example is the Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHR): This is calculated by taking your waist circumference (measure around the smallest part of your waist, just above the belly button) and dividing it by your hip circumference (the widest part of your buttocks).
