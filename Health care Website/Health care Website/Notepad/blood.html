BP

High blood pressure is extremely common in Asians Indians.

Want to know why?

Well Sedentary lifestyle, unhealthy diet, and a family history of high blood pressure are a few contributing factors. Excess weight around the stomach is a visible indicator of high blood pressure risk and is very common in Asian Indian men.

Normal Range?

Top number (Systolic) in mm Hg Bottom Number (diastolic)in mm Hg Your Category What should you do?
Below 120 AND Below 80 Normal Blood pressure Maintain and Adopt healthy LifeStyle
120-139 OR 80-89 PreHypertension Maintain and Adopt a Healthy lifeStyle
140-159 OR 90-99 Stage 1 Hypertension Maintain and adopt to healthy lifestyle.If doesn't reach normal in 6 months .then consult your doctor.
160 OR Above OR 100 OR Above Stage 2 Hypertension Maintain or adopt To the healthy life style and immediately consult the doctor

How should I check?

Remember that high blood pressure in most people does not cause any symptoms. That is why it is called a silent killer. Although regular visits to see your doctor are important, monitoring your blood pressure at home is the best way to keep track of your numbers. Be sure to purchase an arm cuff made by a reliable manufacturer. Note: Monitor your own blood pressure and report results to doctor when numbers are not within the normal range.

Blood Sugar

A blood glucose test measures the amount of a type of sugar, called glucose, in your blood. Glucose comes from carbohydrate foods. It is the main source of energy used by the body. Insulin is a hormone that helps your body's cells uses the glucose.

Normal Ranges:

  • Blood sugar levels over 200 mg/dL or under 60 mg/dL are considered unhealthy.
  • High blood sugar levels (above 200 mg/dL) may be a sign of inadequate levels of insulin.
  • Low blood sugar levels (below 60 mg/dL) may be caused by taking too much insulin.
  • A good blood glucose level is between 60 mg/dL and 120 mg/dL.

How should I check?
There are several different types of blood glucose tests. Fasting blood sugars (FBS), 2-hour postprandial blood sugar test, Oral glucose tolerance test, HbA1c test. These tests are performed by the certified medical professional.

Cholesterol

Cholesterol profile (Triglycerides, LDL, and HDL) of an Asian Indian is different when compared with other ethnic groups. This is due to a combination of high risk genetics, diet and a lack of activity. High cholesterol increases the risk of heart attack and stroke.
    →Triglycerides are another form of cholesterol that can also raise heart attack risk. Triglyceride levels are usually high in Asian Indians.
    →LDL deposits fat onto the inside of your blood vessels leading to heart attacks or strokes. LDL (bad cholesterol) levels tend to be     normal or only mildly elevated in Asian Indians.
    →HDL is the healthy cholesterol that helps sweep the fat away from the inside of your blood vessels. HDL (good cholesterol) levels are     usually low in Asian Indians.
Normal Range:
  • Since South Asians develop heart disease at such a young age, high cholesterol must be brought under control as soon as possible.
  • Triglycerides level should always be less than 150 mg/dL. A level greater than this is considered high.
  • LDL level should always be less than 100 mg/dL.
  • HDL level should be higher than 40 mg/dL for men.

How should I check?
In the Hospitals with the help of a medical professional
