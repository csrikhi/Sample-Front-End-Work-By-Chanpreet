Portion sizes and Meal planning

The amount you eat or drink plays an important role in your energy balance strategy. Most people eat and drink more when served larger portions. Choosing smaller portions can help you lose weight and keep it off. Let us look into simple rules

    Establish meal times that are four to six hours apart. Timing meals and snacks can be challenging with today's busy schedules. If you will be going six or more hours without a meal, plan a snack between meals. Eat every two to three hours, with snacks being small and meals modest in size. Late evening meals (especially heavy dinners) can aggravate reflux, fasting blood sugar, contribute to obesity and depress morning appetite. Aim for breakfast within 30 to 60 minutes of rising and stop eating two to three hours prior to sleeping. A simple way to avoid eating too much is to skip the second helpings. Using smaller plates and bowls can also help keep portions smaller. Most restaurant portions are oversized, but you can ask to have half of your meal wrapped up so you can take it home.

Rules to Control eating:


Rule 1: Aim for breakfast within 30 to 60 minutes of rising and stop eating two to three hours prior to sleeping.

Rule 2: Eat every two to three hours, with snacks being small and meals modest in size.

Rule 3: Using smaller plates and bowls can also help keep portions smaller.

Rule 4: A simple way to avoid eating too much is to skip the second helpings.

Rule 5: Include lots of fiber foods (Fruits and vegetables) to beat the hunger pangs.

Rule 6: Most restaurant portions are oversized, but you can ask to have half of your meal wrapped up so you can take it home or simply share the food and the bill with your friends.

Note: Avoid late evening meals, (especially heavy dinners) because they can aggravate reflux, fasting blood sugar, contribute to obesity and depress morning appetite.
